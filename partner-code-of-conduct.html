Certified Photographer Partner Standards

Partner Code of Conduct

Every Certified Partner represents both an independent photography business and the Signature Story Collection experience. These standards protect clients, photographers, territories, and the reputation of the entire Partner network.

This Code of Conduct supplements the Terms of Service, Partner Agreement, Privacy Policy, and Refund Policy. Certified Partner status depends on following these standards throughout the active certification term.

1. Purpose and Core Standard

Honest Clear pricing, accurate claims, and transparent communication
Professional Reliable service, respectful conduct, and responsible follow-through
Protective Respect for client privacy, photography rights, and territory boundaries

Certified Partners must conduct business in a way that strengthens trust in Signature Story Collection and protects the dignity, privacy, rights, and expectations of every client.

2. Professionalism and Reliability

Certified Partners are expected to:

  • Communicate clearly, respectfully, and within a reasonable time
  • Provide accurate business, contact, territory, and project information
  • Follow through on commitments made to clients and Signature Story Collection
  • Maintain professional photography and client-service standards
  • Deliver client selections, project details, and requested revisions promptly
  • Keep contact and billing information current
  • Address mistakes directly instead of hiding, blaming, or misleading

Repeated failure to communicate, submit required materials, pay invoices, or support active client projects may affect good-standing status.

3. Honest Marketing and Sales Practices

Sell the real value. Do not invent promises.

Partners may explain the emotional, creative, and business value of Signature Story Collection, but may not guarantee results that are not guaranteed in writing.

Partners must not:

  • Misrepresent pricing, fees, turnaround times, or included deliverables
  • Guarantee a particular song style, emotional reaction, profit, or business result
  • Claim guaranteed Google rankings, YouTube views, leads, or audience growth
  • Describe AI-assisted production as entirely handmade or entirely automated
  • Claim ownership, authority, credentials, or territory rights not actually granted
  • Use fake testimonials, reviews, sales numbers, scarcity, or endorsements
  • Pressure, deceive, or exploit vulnerable clients

Suggested retail pricing and profit examples must be presented as examples, not guaranteed income.

4. Signature Story Collection Brand Use

Active Partners receive a limited right to use approved Certified Partner badges, descriptions, marketing materials, and promotional assets.

Partners must:

  • Use only approved logos, badges, names, and program descriptions
  • Preserve required trademarks, wording, and legal disclosures
  • Use the brand only for authorized Signature Story Collection promotion
  • Remove Certified Partner branding when certification ends or is suspended

Partners must not:

  • Alter the logo or badge in a misleading or damaging way
  • Register confusingly similar domains, business names, trademarks, or social handles
  • Present themselves as an owner, employee, franchisee, or legal representative of Signature Story Collection
  • Create unauthorized contracts, guarantees, policies, or commitments using the brand
  • Resell or distribute confidential Partner resources outside the approved business

5. Territory Respect and Fair Competition

Partners must respect the approved protected areas of other Certified Partners.

A Partner may serve clients outside the Partner’s protected area, but may not falsely advertise exclusive Certified Partner status in another Partner’s assigned territory.

Partners must not:

  • Misrepresent or expand the approved territory without written approval
  • Interfere with another Partner’s client relationship or active project
  • Copy another Partner’s page, branding, portfolio, or local business information
  • Use misleading local pages or false addresses to claim unapproved markets
  • Harass, undermine, threaten, or disparage another Partner

Territory concerns should be reported directly to Signature Story Collection rather than argued publicly or with clients.

6. Client Communication and Service Standards

The photographer remains responsible for the customer-facing relationship, including pricing, deposits, refunds, session agreements, image delivery, and communication.

Partners should clearly explain:

  • What the standard collection includes
  • That the service uses a personalized custom-written song and cinematic photo story video
  • That production may use premium licensed AI-assisted creative tools
  • That 1–20 finished professional photographs are included
  • That one reasonable revision round is included
  • The difference between public and unlisted YouTube delivery
  • The Partner’s own retail price, payment schedule, and refund terms

Partners must not conceal material terms or blame Signature Story Collection for promises the Partner independently made.

7. Client Privacy and Confidentiality

Client stories, photographs, contact information, family details, project files, and private communications must be handled with reasonable care.

Partners must:

  • Collect only information reasonably needed for the client relationship and project
  • Use client information only for authorized business and production purposes
  • Protect private links, forms, files, passwords, and contact information
  • Avoid sending sensitive client information through insecure or unauthorized channels
  • Follow the selected public or unlisted publication preference
  • Report suspected privacy, security, or unauthorized-access issues promptly

Client information may not be sold, traded, posted, or used for unrelated marketing without proper authorization.

8. Photographs, Stories, and Legal Permissions

Partners may submit only photographs, logos, stories, names, and materials they are authorized to provide.

The Partner is responsible for obtaining any required:

  • Client consent
  • Copyright or license permission
  • Model or publicity release
  • Parental or guardian authorization
  • Public or unlisted YouTube publication permission
  • Permission to display names, logos, watermarks, and business credits

Partners must not knowingly submit stolen, infringing, deceptive, defamatory, exploitative, or unlawful materials.

9. Payments, Billing, and Financial Conduct

Partners must pay production fees and annual certification fees according to the applicable agreement and invoice terms.

Partners must:

  • Provide accurate billing information
  • Pay the first five project invoices within 72 hours after delivery unless otherwise approved
  • Pay approved Net 30 invoices by the stated due date
  • Raise billing questions promptly and in good faith
  • Contact Signature Story Collection before filing a chargeback when reasonably possible

Fraudulent disputes, intentional nonpayment, repeated late payment, or misuse of chargebacks may result in loss of billing privileges, suspension, territory reopening, collection activity, or termination.

10. Project Submission and Quality Standards

Partners are responsible for submitting complete, accurate, and organized project information.

Before production, the Partner should confirm:

  • The client completed the correct story form
  • Names, dates, relationships, and important details are accurate
  • The client selected between 1 and 20 final edited photographs
  • The submitted files are the intended final versions
  • The publication preference is clear
  • Anything to include or avoid has been properly submitted

Revision requests should be clear, respectful, specific, and consolidated into one reasonable round whenever possible.

11. Respectful and Safe Conduct

Signature Story Collection does not tolerate:

  • Harassment, threats, intimidation, stalking, or abuse
  • Discrimination or hateful conduct
  • Sexual harassment or exploitation
  • Retaliation against a client, Partner, contractor, or reporter
  • Public attacks intended to damage another Partner or the network
  • Fraud, theft, impersonation, or deliberate deception
  • Pressure to create unlawful, dangerous, or seriously harmful content

Legitimate criticism, complaints, and disagreements may be raised professionally through the appropriate review process.

12. Complaints, Mistakes, and Conflict Resolution

Partners should address problems early, directly, and in good faith.

When a client raises a concern, the Partner should:

  1. Listen and document the concern accurately
  2. Separate photography issues from Signature Story Collection production issues
  3. Avoid promising a refund, remake, or resolution on behalf of Signature Story Collection without approval
  4. Send one clear summary with relevant project information
  5. Allow a reasonable opportunity for correction or review

Partners must not fabricate complaints, conceal relevant information, or manipulate clients into making false claims.

13. Reporting Concerns and Violations

A Partner should promptly report suspected:

  • Unauthorized use of the Signature Story Collection brand
  • False Certified Partner claims
  • Territory misrepresentation
  • Client privacy or security incidents
  • Fraud, abuse, threats, or illegal activity
  • Copyright or permission concerns
  • Serious violations of this Code

Reports should include enough factual information to allow a fair review. Knowingly false or malicious reports are themselves a violation.

Signature Story Collection will handle reports as discreetly as reasonably possible but cannot guarantee complete confidentiality when investigation, safety, fairness, or law requires disclosure.

14. Review, Corrective Action, and Enforcement

Conduct concerns may be reviewed based on their seriousness, frequency, evidence, client impact, payment status, cooperation, and risk to the Partner network.

Possible actions include:

  • Informal guidance or clarification
  • A written warning
  • Required correction, training, or action plan
  • Removal of misleading marketing or unauthorized brand use
  • Temporary suspension of Partner benefits or production
  • Loss of Net 30 billing privileges
  • Suspension or reopening of the protected territory
  • Termination of Certified Partner status
  • Referral to payment providers, platforms, insurers, attorneys, or authorities when appropriate

Serious misconduct may result in immediate suspension or termination without progressive warnings.

Expiration, suspension, or termination requires removal of Certified Partner badges and claims. Outstanding payment, confidentiality, intellectual-property, and legal obligations remain enforceable.

Certified Partner Acknowledgment

Protect the Client. Protect the Craft. Protect the Network.

By accepting Certified Partner status, the Partner agrees to follow this Code of Conduct together with the Partner Agreement, Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, and Refund Policy.
